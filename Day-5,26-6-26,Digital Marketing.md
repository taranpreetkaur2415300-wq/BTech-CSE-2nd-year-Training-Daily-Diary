# WordPress
## Introduction
WordPress is a free, open-source Content Management System (CMS) that powers over 40% of all websites on the internet. 
It allows users to build, manage, and scale websites—from simple personal blogs to complex e-commerce stores—without 
needing advanced coding knowledge.
## The Two Versions of WordPress
### WordPress.org
It is also known as self-hosted WordPress. This is the 100% free, open-source software where you download the files 
and maintain full ownership of your site.You just need to purchase a separate domain and web hosting.
### WordPress.com
A for-profit hosting service created by the co-founder of WordPress. It simplifies the setup process but requires premium paid 
plans to unlock advanced features like custom plugins.
## Core Components of a WordPress Site
### Posts
Dynamic, chronological content usually associated with a blog or news feed.
### Pages
Static content for permanent sections of your site, like an "About Us" or "Contact" page.
### Dashboard
The administrative backend where you manage content, install updates, and configure website settings.
